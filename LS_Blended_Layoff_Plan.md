## Executive Summary

This report presents a specific, position-level plan to achieve **$13 million in total salary cost savings** ($10 million in listed salaries + $3 million in associated overhead) across the College of Letters & Science. The blended approach prioritizes eliminating high-paid management and administrative positions while completely protecting front-line staff -- researchers, lecturers, teaching faculty, lab technicians, advisors, and support staff.

## Plan Structure

The plan proceeds in six phases, starting with the highest-paid positions and working down. Each phase has a clear strategic rationale.

## Phase 4: Small Department Administrator Sharing

**Consolidate department administrators across small humanities departments that currently each maintain a dedicated admin.**

| # | Department | Title | Salary | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 33 | Political Science | Dept Administrator II | $142,617 | Replace with shared Dept Admin I |
| 34 | Anthropology | Dept Administrator I | $109,776 | Share admin with Classical Studies |
| 35 | Art History | Dept Administrator I | $101,529 | Share admin with nearby dept |

**Phase 4 total: 3 positions, $353,922 saved | Running total: $6,361,792**

**Rationale:** Several small humanities departments (5-11 non-faculty staff) each have a dedicated department administrator earning $100-143K. Under this plan:

- **Anthropology** (5 non-faculty) and **Classical & Ancient Near Eastern Studies** (5 non-faculty) would share a single Department Administrator I. Both are small humanities units in the same building cluster with similar administrative needs.
- **Art History** (5 non-faculty) would share an administrator with another small humanities department (e.g., Religious Studies, African Cultural Studies, or Language Sciences -- all with similar scale).
- **Political Science** (11 non-faculty) currently has a Department Administrator II at $142,617 -- the highest-paid dept admin relative to unit size. This role would be replaced by a shared Department Administrator I position (not included in savings since it's a new cost; net savings reflect the eliminated position only).

Note: The shared administrators replacing these roles would need to be hired or reassigned. The salary figures above represent gross savings from eliminating the current positions. Net savings would be reduced by the cost of shared replacements, but given that one administrator would serve two departments instead of each having a dedicated one, the net savings are approximately 50% of gross. The plan accounts for this by carrying a small surplus ($141K) that provides a buffer for replacement costs.

## Implementation Risks and Mitigations

### Risk 1: L&S Administration capacity collapse
**Impact:** 26 of 82 management positions eliminated (32%). The remaining 56 managers must absorb additional responsibilities.
**Mitigation:** Phase implementation over 12 months. Use the transition period to redistribute portfolios, automate administrative processes where possible, and identify which eliminated functions can be discontinued entirely vs. reassigned.

### Risk 2: Loss of institutional knowledge
**Impact:** Senior leaders (associate deans, directors) hold deep knowledge of university processes, accreditation requirements, and external relationships.
**Mitigation:** Require 90-day transition periods for Phase 1-2 positions. Document critical processes and relationships before departures. Prioritize knowledge transfer to retained staff.

### Risk 3: Emeritus non-renewal backlash
**Impact:** Professor Emeritus appointees are often prominent scholars whose ongoing association benefits the university's reputation.
**Mitigation:** Frame as appointment non-renewal (a routine administrative action), not termination. Offer emeritus title retention without salary. Provide continued access to office space and libraries where cost is minimal.

### Risk 4: Shared department administrator model failure
**Impact:** Departments accustomed to dedicated administrators may experience service degradation.
**Mitigation:** Pilot the shared model with Anthropology/Classical Studies before extending to Art History. Set clear service-level expectations. Provide the shared administrator with authority over both departments' administrative workflows.

### Risk 5: Student Academic Affairs service reduction
**Impact:** Reducing from 12 to 8 assistant deans increases caseloads for remaining deans.
**Mitigation:** The remaining 8 assistant deans still provide a generous staffing ratio relative to other units. Invest in self-service advising tools and peer advising programs to offset capacity loss.

## Limitations

1. **Salary figures are from September 2025.** Actual current salaries may differ due to raises, departures, or new hires since the data snapshot.
2. **Individual identification.** This report identifies positions by title, department, and salary -- not by name. Final implementation requires HR verification of specific individuals.
3. **Contractual constraints.** Some positions (especially Limited Appointees) may have contract terms that affect the timing or cost of elimination. Severance, accrued leave payouts, and other separation costs are not included in savings figures.
4. **Funding source.** Some positions may be funded by external grants or endowments rather than state funds. Eliminating a grant-funded position would not generate state budget savings.
5. **Replacement costs for shared administrators** (Phase 4) are not fully modeled. The plan's $141K surplus provides a partial buffer, but actual net savings from shared positions depend on the salary of replacement hires.
6. **Emeritus non-renewals** may not all be achievable immediately if current appointments have remaining terms. Savings would phase in as appointments expire.
